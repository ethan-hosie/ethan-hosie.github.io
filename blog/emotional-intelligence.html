Emotional intelligence

What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the ability to manage your own emotions and be aware of the emotions of others around you. from a very young age we are learning to develop our emotional intelligence through the trials and tribulations of our toddlerism, not sure if that's an official name for that period in our lives but it comes with so many challenges which, mean that from a really early age we are navigating emotional intelligence by learning to regulate our emotions and show empathy. To build our emotional intelligence we need to be able to understand our own emotions in positive ways, the emotions of others and show empathy.

How is EQ different to IQ?

Eq - emotional quotient, is a measurement of your emotional intelligence. It measures your ability to regulate your emotions, ability to self motivate, communicate emotions and recogise emotions in others. However, IQ - intelligence quotient measure your capability to learn, understand and apply knowledge to everyday tasks. Some aspects taken into consideration when measuring IQ are, how well you are able to think logically, communicate, problem solve and comprehend information.

Why is EQ important?

Emotional intelligence is important because it supports us in acheiving our goals and building relationships. Emotional intelligence allows us to understand a range of feelings as we move through our lives and supports us to respond to events in our environments including family, work and the world.
